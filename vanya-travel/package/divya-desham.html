Divya Desham

Divya Deshams hold immense significance in the Hindu tradition, particularly within the Vaishnavism sect. These 108 sacred temples, revered by the Alvars (devotee poets), are spread across India and are celebrated for their spiritual and historical importance. Each temple is considered a divine abode of Lord Vishnu, and visiting these sites is believed to bring immense spiritual merit and blessings. The Divya Deshams not only serve as centers of worship but also as repositories of rich cultural heritage, preserving ancient hymns, rituals, and architectural styles. They play a crucial role in sustaining the Vaishnavite traditions and fostering a deep connection between devotees and the divine.
